What's actually inside a PC
A plain-English rundown of the parts you'll see in each listing's spec sheet
CPU
The processor — handles general computing. More cores helps with multitasking and heavy workloads; higher clock speed helps with responsiveness and gaming.
GPU
The graphics card — renders images and video. Essential for gaming, video editing, and 3D work. VRAM (video memory) matters more at higher resolutions.
RAM
Short-term memory the system uses while running. More RAM means smoother multitasking and fewer slowdowns with heavy apps open.
Storage
Where your files and programs live long-term. NVMe SSD is much faster than a traditional hard drive — quicker boot times and load times.
Motherboard
Connects every component together. Determines what CPU/RAM is compatible and how many upgrade options you'll have later.
PSU
Power supply unit — feeds power to every part. A quality PSU protects your build and supports future upgrades.
